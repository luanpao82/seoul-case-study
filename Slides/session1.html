Final Research Project 40%

Choose one format. Both require a comparative analysis of an SMG policy or management issue with U.S. practices.

Option A: Written Report

  • 15–20 pages, double spaced, APA 7th
  • Literature review with 15–20 scholarly sources
  • Methodology, findings, policy implications

Suggested outline: Title & Executive Summary (10%) → Introduction (10%) → Literature Review (focus on governance) → Methodology → Findings & Conclusion → References

Option B: Video Report

  • 15–20 min unlisted YouTube video
  • Must include field footage from Seoul
  • Narration and your face on camera required
  • Sections: Intro (2–3 min) → Lit Review (3–5 min) → Seoul Case (5–7 min) → U.S. Comparison (3–4 min) → Conclusion (1–2 min)
  • Submit with a 2–3 page supplementary doc (APA references, 10+ sources, 250-word abstract)
Due: 11:59 PM, Friday, June 20, 2026 via Webcourses

Late projects will not be accepted. Videos and written reports may be published on the course website. Evaluated on: content & analysis (40%), use of Seoul field experience (20%), organization & clarity (15%), scholarly rigor (15%), production quality (10%).

Korean Language

Korean Language?

Sentence Structure

S + O + adj + … adv + V!

Example:
"I pizza eat" instead of "I eat pizza"
나는 피자를 먹는다

Intonation / Accent

  • Monotone
  • Depending on your mood

Very Hierarchical Language

  • Word choices show social respect
  • Age is a very important determinant

Hangul (한글)

ㅂ ㅅ ㅇ ㅈ ㅎ

Korean Language

Language Hierarchy

Level 1 Casual

Use with friends or younger. Friend level, defined by school year (K1).

Level 2 Polite / Informal

Informal setting. Acquaintances: same age, younger, or older.

Level 3 Formal

Business meeting, military, conference presentations… Always safe to use this one.

Culture

S. Korea

Very small, not many resources, Japanese colonial, Korean war, dictatorship, and Korean style democracy…

  • Very strong desire to success (investment in education)
  • Competition everywhere — just do it, do it well & very fast
  • Everybody is busy working

Confucianism vs Modern Culture

  • Very communal and collectivistic
  • Strong social norm, hierarchical
  • Also very strong individualism
  • Do not bother others

Culture

S. Korea has its own things,
just like all other countries

Please do not feel offended by:

People asking your age
People walking fast and overtaking without saying "Excuse me"
People slightly pushing you on the street or in the subway
People not holding doors for you
People staring at you
People not smiling back to you
People covering their mouths when they laugh/speak
People not making eye contact when talking to you
PAD 6958 · Summer 2026
Culture

When you travel to other countries…

Do NOT use your U.S. framework / standard

But, try to understand or at least to see.

  • Everybody is different/unique — please do not stereotype people, culture, or social/political system
  • Remember, your experience is just a sample of one — do not generalize your opinion
  • Be open minded, and respectful
  • If you do not know what to do, observe what people do at the moment and imitate
